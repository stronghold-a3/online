PHASE 1: PRE-CRISIS (Baseline Digital Readiness) MITIGATION STEP

Ensuring personnel, systems, and communications are completely primed before any disaster hits Eastern Visayas.

01

Offline-First Setup

All guards and supervisors operate the customized Cloud SMS app. Preloaded templates, local site instructions, emergency protocols, and contact indices are fully cached natively on mobile devices.

02

Pre-Positioned Kits

Supervisors and zonal hubs are pre-equipped with tactical battery power banks, secondary dual-SIM cards ("Emergency Load"), and emergency disaster kits ("Bayanihan Packs") for rapid deployment.

03

Automated Client Notice

Ops Manager pre-wires critical, localized "Crisis Updates" within the system dashboard. Templates are bound to live client indexes, configured for immediate automated SMS and Viber dispatch.

The Rule of Pre-Positioning:

By pushing critical field database caches and robust communications hardware directly to local terminals before any regional weather/physical system shocks, we neutralize our minute-zero dependency on regional internet infrastructures.

SOP SUCCESS FACTORS & DRILLS MANUAL CONTINUOUS READINESS

Maintaining operational excellence requires strict enforcement of these non-negotiable success parameters.

The "Emergency Load" Protocol

Stronghold A3 equips all guard personnel with dedicated pre-loaded dual-SIM cards (Smart/Globe) reserved only for crisis communication, protecting contact loops if personal data is consumed.

Quarterly Blackout & Breach Drills

Once a quarter, Ops Manager executes simulated infrastructure failure and mock security breaches. Ground personnel must successfully run roll calls and submit offline logs.

Client Expectation Alignment

During task-based 72-Hour onboarding phases, explicitly present this redundant communication and decision matrix to clients: "If a typhoon strikes at 2 AM, our redundant networks and backup protocols will keep you secure and informed."

Frontline Rules of Engagement (ROE)

Frontline personnel must be trained on-site that in active civil unrest, guard safety is the first priority. Guards observe, log, secure access points, and extract if necessary—no tactical vigilante engagement.
