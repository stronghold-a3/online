RULES OF ENGAGEMENT: HOW TO KILL THE BYSTANDER EFFECT OPERATIONAL MANDATES

To ensure this matrix drives immediate behavioral change, leadership must strictly enforce the four operational laws below.

01

The "One A" Rule is Absolute

If a task fails, the person designated with the Accountable (A) badge takes sole ownership of the impact. Responsibility (R) can be shared, but Accountability remains localized to one unique decision node.

02

Consultation is Mandatory

The "A" node must actively consult the C (Consulted) subject matter expert before executing any process. An Ops Manager cannot change site rules without consulting the Zonal Supervisors and Head Guards who live the ground reality.

03

Information is Pushed, Not Pulled

If you hold an Informed (I) status, you are never required to search for status reports. The operational "A" or "R" is hardcoded to automatically push updates via the Cloud SMS dashboard or localized Viber feeds.

04

No "Double Dipping" on 'R'

Where multiple roles share the R (Responsible) tag, team leaders must define explicit split-roles to prevent overlapping efforts, structural gaps, or execution blindness.

Golden Law: If everybody is responsible, nobody is. The single "A" guarantees unambiguous systemic velocity.
